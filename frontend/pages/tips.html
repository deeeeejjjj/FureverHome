The First Week With Your New Pet: A Complete Guide

🐕 Dogs 🐈 Cats 🏠 New Owners 📖 7-Day Guide

Bringing a new dog or cat home is one of the most exciting moments — but those first 7 days set the tone for your entire relationship. This guide walks you through everything you need to know, day by day.

Day 1
Arriving Home — The First Hours

When you first bring your pet home, resist the urge to overwhelm them with attention. Let them explore at their own pace. Set up a quiet, dedicated space — a crate, corner, or room — with their bed, water bowl, and a piece of your worn clothing so they can get used to your scent.

  • Keep the house calm — limit visitors on Day 1
  • Show them where their food, water, and bathroom area are
  • Let them approach you — don't force interaction
  • Speak in calm, soft tones to build trust immediately
Days 2–3
Introducing the Family

Gradually introduce family members one at a time. Children should be taught to approach slowly and quietly. If you have other pets, introduce them through a closed door first so they can smell each other before meeting face to face.

  • Let the new pet eat and rest without disturbance
  • Supervised, brief meetings with other pets — never forced
  • Establish a consistent feeding schedule (same time each day)
  • Begin learning your pet's personality and stress signals
Days 4–5
Building a Routine

Pets thrive on predictability. By days 4–5, start locking in a daily routine for feeding, walks or play sessions, and rest time. Consistency reduces anxiety and helps your pet understand what to expect from their new life.

  • Feed at the same times morning and evening
  • Walk dogs at consistent times — morning, after meals, evening
  • Create a quiet "wind-down" time before bed
  • Start basic commands: sit, come, stay — with treats and praise
Day 6
First Vet Visit

Schedule a vet appointment within the first week even if your pet seems healthy. A baseline health check establishes records, updates vaccinations, and lets your vet catch any issues early — especially important for rescue animals.

  • Bring any vaccination records from the shelter or previous owner
  • Ask about deworming, flea prevention, and microchipping
  • Discuss diet and portion sizes appropriate for their age and breed
  • Ask the vet what signs of illness to watch for in the first month
Day 7
Reflect & Adjust

By the end of the first week, take stock of how things are going. Some pets settle in quickly; others need more time. The 3-3-3 Rule is a helpful reminder: 3 days to decompress, 3 weeks to learn your routine, 3 months to feel truly at home.

  • Note what's working — and what needs adjusting
  • Be patient if your pet is still nervous or hiding
  • Celebrate small wins — trusting you, eating well, playing
  • Remember: every pet adjusts at their own pace. Love and consistency are the keys
Pro Tips for Marinduque Pet Owners
Tropical Heat
Walk dogs early morning or after 5 PM to avoid heatstroke on hot Marinduque days.
Flea & Tick Season
Parasites are year-round here. Start prevention treatment from Day 1.
Hydration
In our warm climate, always have fresh cool water available — check it twice a day.
Local Vets
Contact the Boac Animal Shelter or Gasan PAWS Center for affordable vet referrals.
